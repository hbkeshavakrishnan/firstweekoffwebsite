Daytrips

Goa day tours include North Goa sightseeing tour, South Goa sightseeing tour, House boat day tour, Island Trip and Dudhsagar Waterfall Trip. North Goa tour includes visit to Fort Aguada Jail, Portuguese light house, Sinquerim beach, Calangute beach, Candolim beach, Baga beach, Anjuna beach, Vagator beach and Chapora fort. South Goa day tours includes visit to Old Goa Churches, Mangueshi Temple, trip to spice plantation, Dona Paula, Miramar beach and Sunset River Cruise. On the house boat day tour you are taken to a boat safari through the Chapora River or Mandovi River, it also includes crocodile sighting! Island trip is popular because you are taken to Grande Island where the water is clear and you can do Snorkeling and fishing! You are also served BBQ lunch! Dudhsagar waterfall trip includes trip to Mollem National park, Jeep safari to Dudhsagar and bathing and swimming at the falls, you are also taken to spice plantation for Goa Cuisine!

South Goa

South Goa sightseeing is an amazing and mandatory part of Goa darshan and has a variety of places from waterfalls to beaches to forts and churches! Dudhsagar waterfall literally translates to “Sea of Milk” is surrounded by Mollem National Park and Bhagwan Mahaveer Sanctuary. Margoa is one of busiest bustling cities in south Goa and is a must see in South Goa sightseeing packages. The Holy Spirit church in Margoa is another famous attraction. Colva beach is also known as the, “White Sand” beach in south Goa sightseeing. Turtles make their nests at Galgibaga beach to the south and birds in the Catigao Wildlife Sanctuary inland. Once you reach butterfly beach in south Goa sightseeing trip, you feel like you are in wonderland with millions of butterflies flying over the mountain flowers and the magical dance of the Dolphins!


Along with all these Goa is very famous for temples like Shantadurga temple(One of the richest temples dedicated to Shantadurga the goddess of peace), Mangueshi temple(Dedicate to Lord Shiva, being build in the 17th Century).

North Goa

If you want to visit North Goa sightseeing some of the best places on the maps are the Basilica of Bom Jesus which contains the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier and is in close proximity to Panjim. Mapusa market comes second only to Panjim with its famous, “Mapusa Friday Market”. The 500 year old Harvalem waterfall is located in Bicholim and has a temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Calangute and Baga are called the queen of beaches in Goa. Anjuna and Vagator have recently become famous for hosting the “Sun Burn” festival in Goa and should be on your bucket list for North Goa sightseeing!


A very famous western coastal state Goa is famous for its beautiful beaches across India. Covering as many beaches as possible is very challenging as there are many beaches in Goa. North Goa Tour is aimed to cover some of them in the daytime.

Water sports in Goa

The exciting adventure watersports in Goa are parasailing, Jet Ski ride, Banana ride, Bumper ride, water rafting, and kayaking. Parasailing in Goa – This adventure watersports goa activity in Goa parachute based pulled by speed boat. Jet Ski ride is like riding a bike on the surface of the water. The famous water activity in Goa is the Banana ride. People sit on a banana spaced boat and the speed boat drags it. The bumper ride is a 2 seater boat ride. white Water River Rafting is done during monsoon in Goa. Kayaking is done in the river or lakes in Goa where the water is silent. Dudhsagar waterfalls

Dudhsagar waterfall is one among the tallest falls in India. The fall looks more beautiful as it is surrounded by lush green western ghat. Dudhsagar fall is very close to Bhagwan Mahveer Sanctuary and National park in Mollem. Geographically the fall is between Goa and Karanataka border and officially falls in Sanguem Taluka of Goa. Dudhsagar Waterfall Trip from Goa is one major tourist attraction in India, dudhsagar waterfall distance from goa is approximately 73kms.
